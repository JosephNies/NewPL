Pikstein Lab

Pikstein Laboratory for Conservation Research & Education is dedicated to addressing critical environmental challenges, including biodiversity and habitat loss, invasive species, and other forms of ecological degradation.

The lab advances its mission through ongoing regional, national, and global fieldwork; the publication of impactful scientific research; and active wildlife rehabilitation and rescue efforts. In addition, the lab contributes to the broader scientific community by presenting findings at professional symposiums and conferences.

A key component of the lab's work is education and mentorship, guiding students at all academic levels—from undergraduate through graduate studies—in conservation, ecology, and veterinary medicine. Beyond academia, the lab engages the public through diverse outreach initiatives, offering educational programming at libraries, schools, community events, and even unconventional venues such as comic conventions and reptile expos, fostering a deeper appreciation and “conservation ethic” within communities.
